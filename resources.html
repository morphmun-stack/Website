Position Paper Guidelines

Learn how to write effective position papers that will help you excel in committee sessions.

Structure

  • Header with country name and committee
  • Introduction (2-3 sentences)
  • Country's position on the topic
  • Past actions and policies
  • Proposed solutions
  • Conclusion

Writing Tips

  • Use formal, diplomatic language
  • Include specific examples and statistics
  • Maintain consistency with your country's policies
  • Keep it concise (1-2 pages)
  • Cite sources when possible

Submission

  • Submit via email to your committee chair
  • Deadline: March 10, 2025
  • Format: PDF or Word document
  • File naming: Country_Committee_PositionPaper

Quick Reference Guide

Motions

  • Motion to Open Debate: Requires majority vote
  • Motion to Close Debate: Requires 2/3 majority
  • Motion to Introduce Draft Resolution: Requires 1/5 support
  • Motion to Introduce Amendment: Requires 1/5 support

Speaking Time

  • General Speakers List: 90 seconds
  • Moderated Caucus: 60 seconds
  • Unmoderated Caucus: 20 minutes
  • Introduction of Resolutions: 120 seconds

Voting

  • Procedural Votes: Simple majority
  • Substantive Votes: 2/3 majority
  • Roll Call Vote: When requested by 1/5 of committee
  • Abstentions: Not allowed on procedural matters

Tips & Guidelines

Essential advice for delegates to maximize their MUN experience and performance.

Public Speaking

  • Speak clearly and at a moderate pace
  • Maintain eye contact with the committee
  • Use gestures to emphasize key points
  • Practice your speeches beforehand
  • Stay within the time limit

Negotiation

  • Build alliances early in the conference
  • Listen actively to other delegates
  • Find common ground and compromise
  • Be respectful of different viewpoints
  • Document agreements in writing

Research

  • Research your country's policies thoroughly
  • Understand the historical context
  • Stay updated on current events
  • Use reliable sources for information
  • Prepare statistics and examples

Networking

  • Attend all social events
  • Exchange contact information
  • Follow up after the conference
  • Join MUN alumni networks
  • Share your experiences

First-Time Delegate Guide

Before the Conference

  1. Complete your registration and payment
  2. Download and study your committee's background guide
  3. Research your assigned country thoroughly
  4. Write your position paper
  5. Prepare your opening speech
  6. Pack appropriate business attire

During the Conference

  1. Arrive early for registration and orientation
  2. Introduce yourself to other delegates
  3. Participate actively in all sessions
  4. Take notes during debates
  5. Collaborate on draft resolutions
  6. Attend social events and networking sessions

After the Conference

  1. Reflect on your experience
  2. Connect with fellow delegates on social media
  3. Apply for future MUN conferences
  4. Share your experience with others
  5. Consider joining your school's MUN club

Code of Conduct

Professional Behavior

  • Maintain professional attire throughout the conference
  • Address fellow delegates and chairs respectfully
  • Follow parliamentary procedure and committee rules
  • Arrive on time for all sessions and events
  • Use appropriate language and avoid offensive remarks

Academic Integrity

  • Submit original position papers
  • Cite sources properly in all written work
  • Maintain consistency with your country's policies
  • Avoid plagiarism and academic dishonesty
  • Respect intellectual property rights

Inclusivity & Respect

  • Respect diverse viewpoints and backgrounds
  • Create an inclusive environment for all delegates
  • Report any instances of harassment or discrimination
  • Support fellow delegates, especially newcomers
  • Celebrate cultural diversity and differences
